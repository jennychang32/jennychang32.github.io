Chun-Yi Chang is a fourth-year PhD student in the <a href = "https://stt.natsci.msu.edu" target = "_blank"> Department Statistics and Probability</a> at <a href = "https://msu.edu" target = "_blank"> Michigan State University</a>. She is advised by <a href = "https://chihli.github.io" target = "_blank"> Dr. Chih-Li Sung</a>. Her research interests include _surrogate modeling_, _uncertainty quantification_, and _computer simultation_, with a particular focus on _deep Gaussian processes_ and _machine learning_.

She received her M.S. in Applied Statistics from Michigan State University and her B.B.A in Statistics from National Taipei Univeristy. 

Outside of research, she enjoys playing volleyball, watching dramas and traveling.
